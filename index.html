Australia's Climate Chronicles: A Visual Exploration

Motivation:

This interactive choropleth map visualises monthly average temperatures across Australia from January 2000 to June 2024. Inspired by Melbourne’s particularly harsh winter this year, I was curious to see how temperatures in other regions compared.

The raw temperature data was collected from the Bureau of Meteorology, and the regional boundaries are based on data from Ferocia.

How-to Guide:
  • Hover: Place cursor over a region to reveal its postcode and average temperature for that month. The legend’s colour gradient indicates temperature ranges.
  • Slider: Navigate through months from January 2000 to June 2024. The slider changes colour to reflect seasonal variations, with the label indicating the current season.
  • Play/Pause: Press Play to initiate the animation to observe temperature trends over time. Press Pause to halt the animation to focus on specific periods.
  • Reset: Return to January 2000 with a single click.
  • Zoom: Press + to zoom in, - to zoom out, and ⟳ to reset the map’s scale.
Insights:

The map illustrates a clear and compelling temperature pattern across Australia. Generally, northern regions maintain warmer temperatures throughout the year compared to their southern counterparts.

As winter approaches, the south begins to cool down more rapidly (especially the south-east), with temperatures dropping noticeably earlier than in the north. This early cooling effect is evident in the map’s colour changes. During winter, a noticeable gradient of colour shifts from cooler tones in the south to warmer hues in the north is observed. This pattern suggests that southern areas, including cities like Melbourne, experience colder temperatures sooner and remain cooler for a longer period compared to northern regions.

Seasonally as summer unfolds, the warmer colours gradually move southward, indicating that southern regions warm up more slowly. This temperature trend highlights Melbourne's persistent chill and underscores the appeal of moving to warmer northern locales!
